Research


My research interests include Reinforcement Learning, Machine Learning in healthcare, Quantitative trading, etc.

Reinforcement Learning for Quantitative trading

As my first research project, I studied offline reinforcement learning with the specific research topic of enhancing the reliability of reinforcement learning in the quantitative investment field. Overfitting and poor model stability have long plagued this field, and the paucity and unreliability of transaction data further hurt the model's capacity to converge and learn.

As the primary person in charge of this project, I realized that we shouldn't just use historical data from one stock to train the model, perhaps we can transform our data training method, that is, extract historical data with a specific morphological feature from all stocks to train our model. Thereby, I proposed my innovative idea (Double DRQN + Conventional Shape Filter) to address the stock style switching and data desert problems, significantly improving the reliability of reinforcement learning in the low-frequency trading level quantitative investment. The strategy developed using our model can allow funds below 10 million RMB to obtain an annualized rate of return of 20% in the Chinese A-share market. I independently programmed over 10,000 lines of code for our back-testing system and reinforcement learning experiments. Our project code is open-sourced on GitHub.

Machine Learning in Healthcare

We learn neuro-symbolic skills with goal-conditioned policies from demonstrations and symbolic predicates. The learned skills can be used with bilevel task and motion planning techniques. Machine learning models can detect the strength of relationships between diseases, however, the causal inference between disease pairs is often difficult to judge, and the interpretability of the results obtained by using machine learning models such as Cox regression is frequently insufficient to support the conclusion. I proposed using the binomial distribution test to solve the causal relationship inference problem. I also proposed to introduce the association analysis method to support our results.
